1 · AI Awareness

Understand the possibilities of AI. Organizations and individuals begin exploring AI tools, learning core concepts, and identifying places where newer models genuinely improve throughput or decision quality - not just novelty.

Focus stays on enablement and confidence: pragmatic education, experimentation, and mapping where guardrails belong.

Examples

  • Using Claude or ChatGPT for research drafts and stakeholder communications
  • Learning prompt frameworks that reduce rework loops
  • Exploring departmental pilots that stay reversible

2 · AI Augmentation

Enhance productivity and judgment. AI becomes woven into repeatable workflows - helping analysts, operators, and revenue owners move faster while humans remain accountable.

This is deliberately not “hands-off autonomy.” Tooling complements craft: summarization, comparison, structuring, alerting, drafting - always with checkpoints your culture can trust.

Examples

  • AI-assisted reporting and variance narration against ERP snapshots
  • Workflow automation bridging email, ticketing, ERP, CRM, MES extracts
  • Copilots constrained to sanctioned data paths and approvals

3 · AI Transformation

Embed AI into core operational fabric. At sustained maturity the organization connects systems, aligns governance, refreshes KPIs - and lets models participate in tighter feedback loops backed by observable metrics.

Investment shifts from episodic demos to managed capability: integrations, audits, instrumentation, staffing models, supplier boundaries, lifecycle reviews.

Examples

  • Manufacturing analytics combining MES, quality, tooling, downtime, backlog
  • Predictive and prescriptive workflows with human-in-loop gates
  • Enterprise-wide interoperability across MES ↔ ERP ↔ CRM plus data foundations

Use this framing to align roadmap sequencing: awareness builds literacy, augmentation proves measurable lift, transformation institutionalizes repeatable advantage.
